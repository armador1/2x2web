About this page

In this webpage you can search information about any 2x2 state. In order to reduce the number of states, we take "equivalent" states as only one.

Two states are said to be equivalents if they both can be solved with the same moves from some orientation. Most states have 23 equivalent corresponding to the 23 possible orientation, but there are also states with less. This notion of equivalence allowed us to reduce the number of states from 3674160 to 154608 unique states.

These states were then classified into the main advanced methods for 2x2 speedsolving. For the states that are not a case of any method, we [REDACTED].

The database queries are based on two inputs: which methods you want to include in your results and what methods you want to exclude. Choosing two or more methods simultaneously will give states that are cases of all of them. For example, including CLL and EG2 will result in states that have a CLL layer in one face and a EG2 layer in another one. On the other hand, exclusion works a bit differently, so excluding CLL and EG2 will result in states that are not CLL nor EG2.

Also, about the CBL option, states that are EG are not included in the CBL list unless they have a CBL case in a different axis from the EG layer. For example, the state with scramble R' U2 R F2 U' R' U F2 U' F has a CLL in the U layer and also a CBL in the R layer, so it counts both as a CLL and a CBL.
